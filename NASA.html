Roya Moussapour

In the summer of 2015, I was the recipient of of a grant from the Maine Space Grant Consortium; this grant allowed me to pursue a summer of physics research with NASA at the Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, AL. I worked under Dr. Peter Jenke on developing the science behind a CubeSat gamma-ray detector. Our work was part of the TRYAD mission, aiming to better understand the cause of terrestrial gamma-ray flashes (originating from the earth) as they relate to thunderstorms.

For more information, check out an article summarizing my research in the Bowdoin Daily News, or read below for my final paper.
