Teaching at King Middle School

At Bowdoin, I took a number of courses in the education department that gave me the opportunity to spend significant time in classrooms around Maine. In two of these classes, Teaching and Learning and Curriculum , I spent my Fridays at King Middle School in Portland, ME, assisting my cooperating teacher throughout class and teaching lessons of my own.

King Middle School is an expeditionary learning school where students spend the year pursuing two semester-long expeditions, tying all their coursework together in relation to the expeditions. King has approximately 500 students across grades 6, 7, and 8, and students speak more than 25 languages and come from 17 countries. Every week, I had the chance to challenge my own assumptions about students, learning to appreciate what diverse backgrounds my students brought to the table.

As part of my teaching internship and coursework, I put together a four week long unit on the physics of sound, using a standards-based curriculum model. I planned for such a unit to be implemented in a classroom similar to the my classroom at King Middle School, a technology education classroom with more typical seats and tables up front and a full woodshop in the back of the room. I incorporated aspects of design, technology, and engineering, aiming for student engagement and excitement throughout each class.
